Reviews of Into the Abyss

Reviews of Into the Abyss are posted when available. If you would like to submit a review for consideration of publication at this site please do so by emailing it to Dr. Carlie within the text of your email or as an attached Word document. By emailing me your review you are also giving me permission to put your review, or portions of it, in the review comments below. And thank you for that!

__________

From: Dr. William Larkin, Sociologist
Dr. Larkin's specialties are the sociology of the family and the sociology of childhood.

I have found Into the Abyss to be an incredibly valuable source of information. These volumes provide both a personal account of Dr. Carlie's extensive experience researching street gangs and a tool to access hundreds of research reports on this subject.

I am currently using Into the Abyss as a research aid for students in two of my courses. It allows them to access a broad array of material on the Internet. I strongly recommend Into the Abyss to other faculty as a resource for their students and to anyone interested in the world of street gangs.

From:  Robert C. Walker
Retired Agent in Charge, Drug Enforcement Administration (Columbia, SC)
Retired Coordinator, Security Threat Group Unit, South Carolina Department of Corrections

I have just spent the past several hours quickly going though your book on line. It is wonderful and I will now start reading again to digest all that you have written. Based on what I have already seen and read, I have written a review for the book (see below). I hope it does justice to such an outstanding work.

The ultimate in gang identification resources and information can be summed up in three words - “Into The Abyss.”  Dr. Carlie has created what will probably become known as the gang identification “bible” for those of us who are in the field of gang identification, as well as those who need, or want to learn more about gangs. I am honored that Dr. Carlie chose to include my web site, Gangs OR Us, and one of its pages - "Gang Slang" - as recommended sources for additional gang information.

I am grateful to you for including my work in your book and I am going to include a link to the book, with an appropriate introduction, on my website. I receive between 6K and 8K page visits each day, so I am sure there will be many others who will have the opportunity to see and learn from your efforts.
