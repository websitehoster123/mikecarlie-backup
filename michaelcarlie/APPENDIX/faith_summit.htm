Media Advisory

Attorney General Earley to Hold Faith Summit to Build Virginia’s Future

Forum for Faith Community Leaders to
Share Successful Mentoring Programs

RICHMOND - Attorney General Mark Earley will host a Faith Summit to Build Virginia’s Future on Tuesday, April 13, 1999, at the Holiday Inn Central, 3207 North Boulevard in Richmond. The Summit will begin at 9:30 a.m., with the Attorney General speaking at 12 p.m., and will provide a forum for Virginia’s faith community leaders to share successful mentoring programs and other ideas to help Virginia’s youth reach their full potential in the 21st century.

Attorney General Earley recently launched his statewide volunteer initiative, Virginia’s Future, to recruit mentors for the Commonwealth’s at-risk and underprivileged youth. Also known as "2000 by 2000," the initiative is designed to mobilize 2,000 new volunteers who will serve as mentors, tutors and coaches for children around Virginia.

The Virginia’s Future initiative is an outgrowth of Attorney General Earley’s Task Force on Gangs and Youth Violence, which he formed in April 1998. The Task Force, after eight months of meetings, research and public hearings, adopted a strong set of recommendations to be implemented

by law enforcement, non-profits, educators, and business and faith community leaders. The Task Force recommendations can be accessed on the website above.

The Office of the Attorney General, in addition to recruiting mentors, will serve as clearinghouse to link those who want to volunteer with organizations in their area that provide volunteer opportunities.

Potential mentors can fill out and return an interest card, or call 1-877-206-9526 to volunteer as a mentor. Those volunteers will then be coordinated with local efforts already ongoing.
